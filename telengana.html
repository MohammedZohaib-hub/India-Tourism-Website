CHARMINAR

Charminar is an iconic monument located in the heart of the old city of Hyderabad, India. It was built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the fifth Sultan of the Qutb Shahi dynasty. The monument is known for its distinctive Islamic architecture and is surrounded by bustling markets and bazaars. The Charminar has four minarets, each standing at 56 meters, and is considered to be one of the most recognizable landmarks in India. It is a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of Hyderabad's rich cultural heritage.

BIRLA MANDIR

Birla Mandir, also known as Laxmi Narayan Temple, is a Hindu temple located in the heart of New Delhi, India. It was built by the industrialist Birla family in 1939 and is dedicated to the deities Laxmi (goddess of wealth) and Narayan (Lord Vishnu). The temple is made of white marble and features intricate carvings and sculptures. It is surrounded by lush gardens and offers a panoramic view of the city. The Birla Mandir is a popular tourist attraction and a place of worship for devotees of Lord Vishnu and Laxmi.

KUNTALA

Kuntala is a picturesque waterfall located in the Adilabad district of Telangana state, India. It is situated amidst dense forests and is one of the highest waterfalls in the state, with a height of approximately 147 feet. The waterfall is formed by the Kadam river and is surrounded by scenic hills and valleys. It is a popular tourist destination and attracts a large number of visitors throughout the year. The area around Kuntala is also known for its rich biodiversity, and visitors can spot a variety of flora and fauna in the region.

MECCA MASJID

Mecca Masjid is a grand mosque located in the old city of Hyderabad, India. It was built during the reign of Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah in the late 17th century and is one of the largest mosques in India, with a capacity of approximately 10,000 worshippers. The mosque is named after the city of Mecca, which is the holiest site in Islam, and is known for its impressive architecture and intricate design.It is a popular place of worship and a significant cultural landmark in Hyderabad.

MEDAK

Medak is a historic town located in the Medak district of Telangana state, India. It is known for its impressive Medak Cathedral, which is one of the largest churches in India and features a Gothic Revival style of architecture. The town is also home to several other historic landmarks, including the Medak Fort, which dates back to the 14th century. Medak is surrounded by lush green fields and offers a serene and peaceful environment. It is a popular destination for tourists looking to explore the rich history and culture of Telangana.

RAMAPPA

Ramappa is a small village located in the Palampet Mandal of Jayashankar Bhupalpally district in Telangana, India. It is known for the famous Ramappa Temple, which was built during the rule of the Kakatiya dynasty in the 13th century. The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its intricate carvings and beautiful sculptures. Ramappa village is surrounded by lush green forests and hills, and visitors can enjoy scenic walks and hikes in the area.

RAMOJI FILIM CITY

Ramoji Film City is a massive film studio complex located in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. The studio is spread over 2,000 acres and is the largest integrated film city in the world, according to the Guinness Book of World Records. Ramoji Film City offers a range of facilities and services, including film production studios, sound stages, post-production facilities, and more.

THOUSAND PILLAR TEMPLE

Thousand Pillar Temple is a historic Hindu temple located in Warangal, Telangana, India. It was built during the Kakatiya dynasty in the 12th century and is known for its impressive architecture and intricate carvings. It is called the Thousand Pillar Temple because of the numerous pillars that support the temple structure, each of which is uniquely carved. The temple is a popular tourist attraction and is visited by thousands of people every year.

NEHRU ZOOLOGICAL PARK

Nehru Zoological Park is a popular zoo located in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. It was established in 1959 and covers an area of approximately 380 acres. The zoo is home to over 1,000 animals and birds from various species, including tigers, lions, elephants, giraffes, and many others. Nehru Zoological Park is known for its conservation efforts and research programs aimed at protecting endangered species. The zoo also features a natural history museum, a train ride, and a variety of other attractions for visitors. It is a popular destination for families, school groups, and animal lovers.

ABOUT THE STATE

Telangana is a state located in southern India, formed in 2014 after being separated from Andhra Pradesh. It covers an area of approximately 114,840 square kilometers and has a population of over 35 million people. The state is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and diverse population. Telugu is the official language of the state, and Hyderabad is its capital city. The state of Telangana has a rich history dating back to the ancient period. The region was ruled by various dynasties, including the Satavahanas, Kakatiyas, and the Nizams of Hyderabad. Telangana is home to several famous landmarks and tourist attractions, such as the Charminar, Golconda Fort, Ramappa Temple, and the Thousand Pillar Temple. These landmarks are a testament to the state's rich cultural heritage and historical significance. Telangana is predominantly an agrarian state, with agriculture being the mainstay of the economy. The state is known for producing rice, cotton, and other crops. The government has also made efforts to promote industrial growth in the state, with Hyderabad being a major center for technology and industry. The state has a thriving IT industry and is home to several multinational companies. Telangana is known for its diverse population and cultural traditions. The state is home to several indigenous communities, such as the Gonds, Koyas, and Lambadas. The people of Telangana are known for their hospitality and warm nature. The state celebrates several festivals and events throughout the year, such as Bathukamma, Bonalu, and the Hyderabad Literary Festival. Hyderabad, the capital city of Telangana, is a major center for education, healthcare, and technology. It is home to several world-renowned educational institutions, such as the Indian School of Business, and the International Institute of Information Technology. The city is also known for its rich cuisine, which is a mix of traditional and modern dishes. In conclusion, Telangana is a state with a rich cultural heritage, diverse population, and a thriving economy. The state is known for its historical landmarks, traditional cuisine, and vibrant festivals. With a focus on promoting industrial growth and technology, Telangana is poised to become a major player in the global economy.
